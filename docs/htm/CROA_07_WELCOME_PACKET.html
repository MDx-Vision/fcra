WELCOME TO BRIGHTPATH ASCEND GROUP

Thank you for choosing Brightpath Ascend Group for your consumer rights advocacy needs. This packet will help you understand our process, what to expect, and how to get the most out of our services.

WHAT WE DO

We are consumer rights advocates specializing in identifying and disputing inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information on your credit reports. We work to protect your rights under federal consumer protection laws including:

•       Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) — Your right to accurate credit reports

•       Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) — Your right to debt validation

•       Consumer Financial Protection Regulations — Your right to fair treatment

IMPORTANT: We are NOT a law firm and do NOT provide legal advice. We are consumer advocates who help you exercise your rights under existing consumer protection laws. We cannot guarantee any specific results.

OUR PROCESS: THE BRIGHTPATH METHOD

ROUND

NAME

WHAT HAPPENS

Round 0

Foundation & Protection

We analyze your reports, freeze secondary bureaus to prevent identity theft, correct PII errors, and send collection validation letters.

Round 1

Initial Disputes

We send comprehensive dispute letters to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion challenging inaccurate items.

Round 2

MOV Demands

We demand Method of Verification documentation — forcing bureaus to prove how they verified disputed items.

Round 3

Regulatory Escalation

We file formal complaints with CFPB, FTC, and State Attorneys General when violations are identified.

Round 4

Pre-Arbitration

We send formal pre-arbitration demands documenting violations and potential legal exposure.

Note: Not all cases require every round. Many items are resolved in earlier rounds. We only proceed to later rounds when necessary and when violations warrant escalation.

 

YOUR RESPONSIBILITIES

Your active participation is essential to achieving the best results. Here's what we need from you:

1.     Provide access to your credit monitoring service so we can pull updated reports

2.     Forward ALL mail you receive from credit bureaus, creditors, and collectors within 5 days

3.     Respond to our requests for information promptly

4.     Notify us immediately if your contact information changes

5.     DO NOT dispute items on your own while we are working your case

SETTING REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS

What We CAN Do:

•       Challenge inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information

•       Identify potential FCRA and FDCPA violations

•       Demand verification and documentation from bureaus and furnishers

•       Escalate violations through regulatory and legal channels

What We CANNOT Do:

•       Remove accurate, current, and verifiable information

•       Guarantee any specific results or score improvements

•       Control how quickly bureaus or creditors respond

•       Provide legal advice (we are not attorneys)

ESTIMATED TIMELINE

Most cases take 4-8 months depending on complexity and your responsiveness. Credit bureaus have 30-45 days to respond to each round of disputes. Delays in providing us with mail or information will extend your timeline.
