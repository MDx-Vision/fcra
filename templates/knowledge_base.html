§611 - CRA Disputes
15 U.S.C. §1681i

Primary weapon for credit bureau disputes.

  • CRAs must conduct reasonable reinvestigation within 30 days (+15 if you provide additional info)
  • If unverifiable, they must delete or correct
  • Must pass dispute to furnisher within 5 business days
  • Must provide results and updated report
Key Tactic: Request "description of procedure used" to verify - exposes superficial ACDV checks and sets up stronger §623 escalation.

§611(a)(5)(B) Reinsertions require furnisher certification AND consumer notification within 5 business days.

§623 - Furnisher Duties
15 U.S.C. §1681s-2

Direct disputes with furnishers (banks, lenders, servicers, collectors).

  • Cannot report info they know or should know is inaccurate
  • Must correct and update information
  • Must flag accounts as "disputed" when appropriate
  • Must conduct reasonable investigation after CRA notification
§623(a)(5): Furnishers must report the Date of First Delinquency (DOFD). Missing DOFD = obsolescence window may be wrong = winnable accuracy angle.
§621 - Administrative Enforcement
15 U.S.C. §1681s

Your path to regulators when internal disputes fail.

  • CFPB - Primary federal regulator for consumer complaints
  • FTC - Federal Trade Commission enforcement
  • State AGs - State Attorneys General (often more responsive)
  • OCC - For national banks
  • NCUA - For federal credit unions
When to Use: Pattern of non-responses, "verified" results ignoring your proof, or systemic violations across multiple clients.

Reg Z - Mortgage Servicing
12 C.F.R. §1026.36(c)(1)

Powerful tool for removing mortgage late payments.

  • Servicers must credit payments as of date of receipt
  • If their delay caused late mark or fee = improper reporting
  • Combine with RESPA §2605(e) for payment logs
Winning Argument: "Payment received 04/15/24; servicer cashed 04/16/24; reported 30-days late for April—violates Reg Z credit-as-of-receipt requirement."
RESPA - Error Resolution
12 U.S.C. §2605(e)

Force mortgage servicers to produce evidence and correct errors.

  • Qualified Written Request (QWR) or Notice of Error
  • Servicer must acknowledge within 20 business days
  • Must resolve within 60 business days
Request: Posting logs, suspense-account history, and correction of misapplied payments that led to late reporting.
FDCPA - Debt Collection
15 U.S.C. §1692 et seq.

Governs debt collectors (not original creditors).

  • §1692g: Validation rights - demand they prove the debt
  • Misrepresentation limits - must be accurate
  • Communication limits - cease and desist options
Strategy: If collector can't validate, tradeline must be deleted. Unverifiable collection = removed across all bureaus.
§605B - Identity Theft Blocks
15 U.S.C. §1681c-2

Lawful "credit sweep" for TRUE identity theft victims.

Requirements for valid §605B block:

  • Identity proof (government ID)
  • Valid identity theft report (FTC report or police report)
  • Identification of impostor accounts
  • Statement the information isn't yours
WARNING: A "credit sweep" based on FALSE police reports is ILLEGAL and can be criminal. Only use for actual identity theft victims.
Metro 2® Compliance Condition Codes
Required for proper dispute handling by furnishers

Furnishers must use these codes to properly track dispute status. Failure to use correct codes = violation.

XB - Consumer Disputes (active dispute filed)
XC - Completed Investigation - Consumer Disagrees
XD - Completed Investigation - Consumer Dispute Resolved (data changed)
XE - Completed Investigation - Consumer Dispute Resolved (no change)
XH - Previously Disputed, Now Resolved
XR - Remove Last Compliance Condition Code
Violation Opportunity: If furnisher fails to flag account as "disputed" (XB code) when you dispute, that's a §623 violation for failing to report dispute status.
Code Meaning Violation If Missing
XB Consumer has active dispute Yes - must flag during investigation
XC Investigation done, consumer still disagrees Required after investigation if consumer disagrees
XD Investigation done, data corrected Required when data is changed
XE Investigation done, no change made Required after investigation with no change
Date of First Delinquency (DOFD)
§623(a)(5) Requirement

Critical field for calculating 7-year obsolescence.

  • Furnishers must report DOFD under §623(a)(5)
  • Missing DOFD = reporting period may be calculated wrong
  • Items may be obsolete EARLIER than shown
Winning Angle: Challenge items missing DOFD as incomplete/inaccurate. CRA cannot properly determine obsolescence without it.
e-OSCAR / ACDV Process
What Bureaus Don't Advertise

CRAs route most disputes through e-OSCAR.

  • Automated Consumer Dispute Verification (ACDV) codes
  • Fast but often superficial
  • May not include your supporting documents
  • Furnishers often just "verify" without actual investigation
Tactic: After "verified," request the "method of verification" under §611(a)(6)(B)(iii). This exposes superficial checks and builds case for §621/§§616-617.

Avoiding "Frivolous" Label
§611 Re-Dispute Rules

Re-disputing same item without new grounds can be labeled frivolous.

To re-dispute, you need:

  • New evidence (payment proof, correspondence)
  • New legal theory (wrong DOFD, missing dispute code)
  • Failure in previous investigation (no method of verification)
  • Different violation angle
Valid Re-Dispute Angles:
• DOFD not reported or incorrect
• Dispute code (XB) never applied
• Method of verification not provided
• Reinsertion without notice
7-Year Obsolescence Rule
§605 Reporting Periods

Most negative items must be removed after 7 years.

  • Clock starts from Date of First Delinquency
  • Bankruptcies: 10 years (Ch. 7) or 7 years (Ch. 13)
  • Tax liens: 7 years from payment
  • Judgments: 7 years or statute of limitations
Tip: If DOFD is missing or wrong, the obsolescence date may be calculated incorrectly. Challenge with §623(a)(5).

Evidence Building Checklist
What to collect at each step
  • Billing statements - Prove payment dates
  • Bank/ACH proofs - Show money movement
  • Cancelled checks - Payment evidence
  • Payoff letters - Account closure proof
  • Email correspondence - Communications record
  • USPS certified mail receipts - Proof of delivery
  • Screenshots - Credit report changes
  • Police/FTC reports - Identity theft cases
Pro Tip: Always send disputes via certified mail with return receipt. Electronic disputes through CRA websites don't create same evidence trail.
When to Involve Attorneys
Triggers for legal action
  • Pattern of non-responses or form letters
  • "Verified" results that ignore your documentary proof
  • Clear Reg Z/RESPA violations causing credit harm
  • Identity theft with substantial damage
  • Reinsertion battles (multiple reinsertions)
  • Denial of credit, housing, or employment due to inaccurate info
Willfulness Indicators: Continued reporting after proof of inaccuracy, failure to correct after regulatory complaint, pattern across multiple consumers.

Primary federal regulator for consumer complaints.

  • Handles credit reporting complaints
  • Forces company response within 15 days
  • Public complaint database
  • Can result in enforcement action

State Attorney General
State-level enforcement

Often more responsive than federal agencies.

  • Consumer protection divisions
  • Can investigate local patterns
  • May have state-specific protections
  • Politically motivated to help consumers
Tip: State AGs often respond faster and may have additional state law claims beyond federal FCRA.
OCC

For complaints against national banks.

  • Regulates banks with "N.A." or "National" in name
  • Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, etc.

CROA Compliance Requirements
15 U.S.C. §1679 et seq. - Credit Repair Organizations Act

If you operate a credit repair business, strict compliance is non-negotiable.

Requirement Details Penalty for Violation
Written Disclosure Must provide CROA disclosure BEFORE any contract Voidable contract + damages
Written Contract Must have signed contract with specific terms Voidable contract + damages
3-Day Cancellation Consumer can cancel within 3 business days Voidable contract + damages
No Advance Fees Cannot charge until services fully performed Criminal penalties possible
No False Claims Cannot guarantee results or make untrue statements FTC enforcement + damages
Critical Warning: Never tell clients to lie, file false police reports, or dispute accurate information. These are federal crimes and destroy your business.
Telemarketing Sales Rule (TSR)
16 C.F.R. Part 310

Additional rules if you telemarket credit repair services.

  • Absolute ban on advance fees for credit repair via telemarketing
  • Must disclose material terms before payment
  • Recording requirements in some states
  • Do Not Call compliance
Safe Harbor: In-person sales and some referral arrangements may have different rules. Consult attorney for complex arrangements.
